Pontus Edvard Samani (born 1989) is an independent scholar of Gāthic philosophy and the apophatic theology of time, served by mathematical structuralism and the study of Indo-Iranian nondual traditions as auxiliary disciplines. Their work applies phenomenology, hermeneutics, and discourse analysis as methods for addressing questions of wisdom and telos. Purpose, on this view, is neither designed nor decreed: it emerges, within a naturalistic frame, as cultures evolve toward attractors through the adjacent possible — and the Gāthās and the tantras are two millennia-deep records of that process.
